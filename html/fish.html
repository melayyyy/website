SALTWATER FISH

Saltwater fish thrive in the diverse marine environments of oceans and seas, from vibrant coral reefs to the vast open waters and deep-sea abysses. They are specially adapted to manage salt levels in their bodies through unique gills and kidneys, enabling them to survive in saline conditions. These fish exhibit incredible diversity, ranging from colorful reef dwellers like clownfish and butterflyfish to powerful predators like sharks and tuna. Deep-sea species, such as anglerfish, showcase remarkable adaptations like bioluminescence for life in darkness.
